## Evaluation of Multiplex Secretome Profiling of Inflammatory Mediators as an Assay for Dry Age-Related Macular Degeneration Therapeutics

---

### Assay Overview

This assay strategy employs multiplex Luminex bead-based immunoassays to quantitatively profile the secreted inflammatory mediator landscape in conditioned media collected from stressed human induced pluripotent stem cell-derived retinal pigment epithelium (hiPSC-RPE) monolayers. The assay models the chronic inflammatory secretory phenotype of dysfunctional RPE cells, which is a central feature of dry age-related macular degeneration (AMD) pathogenesis.

**Disease aspect modeled:** The assay captures the functional consequences of RPE–Bruch's membrane stress, specifically the activation of the NLRP3 inflammasome and complement-dependent inflammatory signaling pathways that drive secretion of pro-inflammatory cytokines and chemokines from RPE cells. These pathways are strongly implicated in the progression from early dry AMD to geographic atrophy (GA) (zhang2021pyroptosisanew pages 6-8, copland2018aperspectiveof pages 3-4, trakkides2019oxidativestressin pages 1-6).

**Measurements:** Conditioned media from stressed hiPSC-RPE monolayers are collected and analyzed using a Luminex FlexMAP 3D or equivalent bead-based multiplex platform. The assay simultaneously quantifies multiple secreted analytes including pro-inflammatory cytokines (IL-1β, IL-6, IL-8, IL-18), interferons (IFN-β), complement-associated factors (C3, C5a), chemokines (MCP-1), and growth factors (VEGF) (huang2026stingactivationinduces pages 36-40). Samples are preserved with protease inhibitor cocktails, run in duplicate on 96-well plates with standard curves generated by 5-parameter logistic regression (huang2026stingactivationinduces pages 36-40).

**Biological material:** hiPSC-RPE cells differentiated to form polarized monolayers, ideally including patient-derived lines carrying AMD-associated risk variants (e.g., CFH Y402H), are subjected to disease-relevant stressors such as oxidative stress (H₂O₂), TGF-β1, A2E accumulation with blue light exposure, or cigarette smoke extract (schikora2025maturationdependentcomplementproduction pages 26-29, castro2018concisereviewhuman pages 4-4, huang2026stingactivationinduces pages 36-40).

### Biomedical Evidence

Chronic inflammation driven by NLRP3 inflammasome activation and complement dysregulation is now recognized as a central pillar of dry AMD pathogenesis, strongly supporting the biological rationale for this assay.

**NLRP3 inflammasome activation in RPE:** The NLRP3 inflammasome functions as a cellular stress sensor in RPE cells, responding to multiple AMD-relevant danger signals. Drusen extracts from AMD eyes directly activate the inflammasome through complement factor C1q signaling, leading to caspase-1 cleavage and IL-1β secretion (zhang2021pyroptosisanew pages 6-8). Additional NLRP3 activators include carboxyethylpyrrole (CEP) proteins that accumulate with age in the retina, Alu RNA transcripts present in RPE cells with geographic atrophy, and amyloid-β, a drusen component that promotes inflammasome activation and upregulates IL-1β, IL-6, IL-18, caspase-1, and NLRP3 in retinal tissue (zhang2021pyroptosisanew pages 6-8, copland2018aperspectiveof pages 3-4). While initial inflammasome activation may serve protective functions, sustained activation causes tissue damage implicated in AMD progression (copland2018aperspectiveof pages 3-4).

**Complement dysregulation and endogenous RPE complement:** Landmark genome-wide association studies positioned the complement system at the center of AMD research. Oxidative stress in RPE cells triggers accumulation of endogenous complement proteins (C3, CFH, properdin) and upregulation of complement receptors (CR3, C5aR1) independent of external complement sources (trakkides2019oxidativestressin pages 1-6, trakkides2019oxidativestressincreases pages 1-3). This intracellular complement accumulation activates the NLRP3 inflammasome and promotes increased secretion of pro-inflammatory and pro-angiogenic factors including IL-8 and VEGF through an autocrine mechanism (trakkides2019oxidativestressin pages 1-6). In geographic atrophy eyes, inflammasome products IL-1β and IL-18 are significantly increased in RPE cells compared to controls (cao2016theroleof pages 91-99). RPE cells carrying the CFH Y402H high-risk polymorphism show increased lysosomal dysfunction and higher internalization of the terminal complement complex C5b-9 (falcao2025targetinglysosomaldysfunction pages 4-5).

**Polarized RPE secretion and disease communication:** The RPE secretes cytokines, chemokines, and growth factors in a polarized manner—apically toward photoreceptors and basally toward the choroid. When stimulated with inflammatory cytokine mixtures (IL-1β, TNF-α, IFN-γ), RPE cells dramatically alter their polarized secretion of chemokines including MCP-1, IL-8, IL-6, RANTES, IP-10, and GRO-α (shi2021controlofchemokine pages 1-2). The STING pathway has been identified as an orchestrator of immune-mediated retinal degeneration, driving polarized cytokine secretion from AMD patient-derived iPSC-RPE: apical IFN-β triggers photoreceptor apoptosis while basal IL-17A compromises choroidal vasculature (huang2026stingactivationinduces pages 36-40). Pathological stressors alter RPE secretion patterns, increasing pro-angiogenic factors like VEGF while reducing anti-angiogenic factors like PEDF and CFH (donato2023humanretinalsecretome pages 10-11).

**Clinical validation of inflammatory and complement pathways:** The complement pathway is clinically validated as a therapeutic target in dry AMD/GA. Pegcetacoplan (C3 inhibitor) demonstrated an approximately 30% reduction in GA progression rate in the Phase 2 FILLY study and was subsequently approved by the FDA (lashkari2020plasmabiomarkersof pages 2-3). Avacincaptad pegol (C5 inhibitor) also showed promising results (patel2022targetingthecomplement pages 14-16). Complement biomarkers including C3a, Ba, Bb, and C5a are elevated in plasma of GA subjects and are used to monitor therapeutic response in clinical trials (lashkari2020plasmabiomarkersof pages 2-3). Serum IL-6 levels have been correlated with geographic atrophy (oladele2026theroleof pages 4-5), and IL-8 haplotypes are associated with AMD development (shafi2024cytokinedrivennfîºbactivation pages 32-35).

### Previous Use

**Direct precedent with iPSC-RPE and Luminex:** Huang et al. (2026) employed the Luminex FlexMAP 3D system to measure cytokine concentrations in conditioned media from AMD patient-derived iPSC-RPE cells, demonstrating polarized cytokine secretion patterns associated with STING pathway activation. This study used the multiplex approach to identify apical IFN-β and basal IL-17A as pathogenic mediators, and further demonstrated therapeutic rescue with the pharmacological STING inhibitor SN-011 (huang2026stingactivationinduces pages 36-40). Schikora et al. (2025) used MILLIPLEX Human Complement panels with Luminex xMAP technology to profile complement component secretion from RPE cells under different maturation states (schikora2025maturationdependentcomplementproduction pages 44-47).

**RPE secretome characterization under stress:** Trakkides et al. (2019) demonstrated that oxidative stress in ARPE-19 cells increases endogenous complement-dependent inflammatory and angiogenic responses, including elevated NLRP3 expression, FOXP3 induction, and increased IL-8/VEGF secretion—all measured through secretome analysis (trakkides2019oxidativestressin pages 1-6, trakkides2019oxidativestressincreases pages 1-3). Schäfer et al. (2020) used RPE secretome profiling to evaluate the effects of properdin, a complement stabilizer, on stressed primary RPE cells, showing that exogenous properdin decreased levels of inflammatory transcripts including NLRP3 and IL-1β in oxidatively stressed RPE (malih2023choroidalmastcells pages 15-16). Meyer et al. (2019) performed comprehensive proteome and secretome analysis of human stem cell-derived RPE under chronic ROS stress, identifying over 1,000 secreted proteins and revealing significant changes including a 4-fold decrease in APOE and increases in TGF-β and urotensin-II (meyer2019proteomeandsecretome pages 1-2).

**Clinical biomarker studies:** Luminex-based multiplex assays have been extensively used in clinical AMD biomarker research. Lashkari et al. (2020) used a 320-analyte Luminex library to identify plasma biomarkers associated with geographic atrophy, identifying 56 changing analytes including amyloid pathway and complement-related markers (lashkari2020plasmabiomarkersof pages 21-22, lashkari2020plasmabiomarkersof pages 2-3). Liu et al. (2021) used Luminex 200 systems to compare intraocular cytokine levels in choroidal neovascularization secondary to different retinopathies, measuring VEGF, IL-6, IL-8, IL-10, IP-10, and MCP-1 (shi2021controlofchemokine pages 1-2).

**Drug evaluation applications:** Polarized RPE soluble factors (PRPE-SF) produced from hESC-derived RPE were characterized through secretome analysis and demonstrated critical antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, preserving retinal structure and function in the Royal College of Surgeons rat model of retinal degeneration (ahluwalia2023polarizedrpesecretome pages 1-2, ahluwalia2023polarizedrpesecretome pages 22-23). While no large-scale drug screening campaigns using this exact Luminex-based iPSC-RPE secretome format have been published, the approach has been used for targeted compound evaluation and mechanistic pharmacology studies.

**Summary assessment:** The Multiplex Secretome Profiling of Inflammatory Mediators assay is a mechanistically well-grounded and translationally relevant approach for evaluating dry AMD therapeutics. Its principal strengths lie in the strong biological rationale connecting RPE inflammatory secretion to disease pathogenesis, the clinical validation of complement and inflammasome pathways as therapeutic targets in geographic atrophy, and the established use of Luminex-based multiplex technology with iPSC-RPE conditioned media in the AMD research field. The use of hiPSC-RPE monolayers provides a more physiologically authentic cell model compared to immortalized cell lines such as ARPE-19, with the added advantage of incorporating patient-specific genetic risk architecture (schikora2025maturationdependentcomplementproduction pages 26-29, yang2022pluripotentstemcells pages 6-8, bhandari2025integrativelandscapeof pages 14-16).

However, important limitations must be acknowledged. First, dry AMD pathogenesis extends well beyond inflammatory secretion to encompass lysosomal dysfunction, mitochondrial impairment, drusen biogenesis, extracellular matrix remodeling, and cell death pathways (basyal2024antioxidantsandmechanistic pages 4-5, basyal2024antioxidantsandmechanistic pages 5-7, meyer2019proteomeandsecretome pages 1-2). A secretome-only assay may therefore miss compounds acting on these parallel pathogenic mechanisms. Second, clinical experience with complement-targeted therapies in GA has shown that modulating inflammation alone often yields modest benefit, with some prominent clinical trial failures (e.g., lampalizumab Phase 3, CLG561) alongside approvals with moderate effect sizes (patel2022targetingthecomplement pages 9-10, lashkari2020plasmabiomarkersof pages 2-3, skerka2026anewperspective pages 12-13). Third, the hiPSC-RPE production pipeline is time-consuming and costly, making this assay better positioned as a secondary/translational profiling tool rather than a primary high-throughput screen (yang2022pluripotentstemcells pages 6-8, bhandari2025integrativelandscapeof pages 14-16).

**Recommendation:** This assay is best deployed as a mechanistically informative secondary screen within a multiparametric assay cascade. It should be paired with complementary readouts such as transepithelial electrical resistance (barrier function), phagocytosis capacity, mitochondrial health, and cell viability to provide a comprehensive evaluation of therapeutic candidates. Its greatest value is in characterizing compounds that normalize the inflammatory secretory phenotype of stressed RPE and in providing translational pharmacodynamic readouts that bridge to clinically measured complement and inflammatory biomarkers in dry AMD patients.

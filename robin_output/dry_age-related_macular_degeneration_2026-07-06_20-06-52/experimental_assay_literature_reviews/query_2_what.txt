Query: What high-throughput, physiologically relevant in vitro cell culture models—such as human induced pluripotent stem cell-derived RPE monolayers or organ-on-a-chip systems—best replicate the metabolic stressors, mitochondrial dysfunction, and oxidative damage observed in the aging macula to allow for the kinetic monitoring of therapeutic efficacy?

## High-Throughput, Physiologically Relevant In Vitro Models for Replicating Aging Macula Pathology and Enabling Kinetic Therapeutic Monitoring

Age-related macular degeneration (AMD) involves a complex interplay of metabolic stress, mitochondrial dysfunction, oxidative damage, and inflammatory signaling in the retinal pigment epithelium (RPE) and outer retina. A growing suite of in vitro platforms—ranging from automated human induced pluripotent stem cell (hiPSC)-derived RPE monolayers to multi-tissue organ-on-a-chip devices—has been developed to recapitulate these pathological features while enabling kinetic readouts for therapeutic screening. The following synthesis evaluates the principal model classes and their monitoring capabilities.

### 1. Automated hiPSC-Derived RPE Monolayers with Seahorse Bioenergetic Profiling

The most direct approach to capturing mitochondrial dysfunction in an AMD-relevant context is the culture of patient-derived hiPSC-RPE on automated platforms coupled with Seahorse extracellular flux analysis. Truong et al. demonstrated an end-to-end automated workflow on a TECAN Fluent workstation, culturing hiPSC lines from multiple AMD donors and differentiating them into RPE, followed by drug testing in 96-well Seahorse XF plates (truong2021automatinghumaninduced pages 6-8, truong2021automatinghumaninduced pages 14-20). The Cell Mito Stress Test protocol measures oxygen consumption rate (OCR) following sequential injections of oligomycin, FCCP, and rotenone/antimycin A to quantify basal respiration, ATP-linked respiration, and maximal respiratory capacity. Eighteen "mitoactive" compounds were screened at 10 µM for 48 hours, with OCR data normalized to cell count post-assay, enabling donor-specific pharmacological profiling of mitochondrial function (truong2021automatinghumaninduced pages 6-8, truong2021automatinghumaninduced pages 14-20). This platform offers high throughput and reproducibility but is limited to two-dimensional RPE without multicellular or structural context.

### 2. Nutrient Microenvironment-Conditioned iPSC-RPE Monolayers

Lim et al. systematically demonstrated that nutrient microenvironment composition is a critical determinant of iPSC-RPE metabolic phenotype and AMD-relevant features (lim2026nutrientmicroenvironmentsreprogram pages 1-5, lim2026nutrientmicroenvironmentsreprogram pages 13-17). Six distinct media formulations were tested, revealing that B27 supplementation enhanced basal and ATP-linked mitochondrial respiration, while human plasma-like medium with FBS (HPLM+FBS) induced prominent lipid droplet accumulation, sub-RPE deposits, and the lowest respiratory activity—features relevant to modeling early dry AMD pathology (lim2026nutrientmicroenvironmentsreprogram pages 21-24). Transepithelial resistance (TER, measured by EVOM2) ranged from below 200 to approximately 800 Ω·cm² depending on media conditions, providing a functional readout of barrier integrity (lim2026nutrientmicroenvironmentsreprogram pages 13-17). Live-cell lipid staining with Lipi-Deep Red and BODIPY, polarized ELISA for VEGF, complement factor H, and apolipoprotein E secretion, and Seahorse XF Pro analysis of both OCR and extracellular acidification rate (ECAR) collectively enable kinetic and endpoint monitoring of metabolic and disease-relevant outputs (lim2026nutrientmicroenvironmentsreprogram pages 29-33, lim2026nutrientmicroenvironmentsreprogram pages 5-9, lim2026nutrientmicroenvironmentsreprogram pages 17-21).

### 3. Aged hiPSC-RPE Organoids (RPEorg) with Drusen Formation

Long-term three-dimensional culture of hiPSC-derived RPE organoids (RPEorg) for up to 360 days recapitulates hallmarks of aging and early AMD that are inaccessible in short-term two-dimensional cultures (mesch2021agedhumanipscrpe pages 1-4, mesch2021agedhumanipscrpe pages 4-7). Mesch et al. showed that aged RPEorg exhibit progressively increased autofluorescence (suggestive of lipofuscin accumulation), neutral lipid accumulation, hydroxyapatite calcification, collagen IV-positive basement membrane formation resembling Bruch's membrane, and extracellular deposits of drusen-associated proteins including APOE, TIMP3, vitronectin, C3, and CFH (mesch2021agedhumanipscrpe pages 7-9, mesch2021agedhumanipscrpe pages 20-22). Electron microscopy reveals drusen-like ultrastructural deposits beneath the outer RPE cell layer (mesch2021agedhumanipscrpe pages 22-27). While these organoids provide a powerful longitudinal aging model, their monitoring is primarily based on serial histological, immunofluorescence, and ultrastructural endpoints rather than continuous real-time biosensing, and throughput is constrained by the long maturation period (mesch2021agedhumanipscrpe pages 1-4, mesch2021agedhumanipscrpe pages 4-7).

### 4. Retina-on-a-Chip (RoC) Integrating iPSC Organoids and RPE

The retina-on-a-chip (RoC) developed by Achberger et al. represents a landmark microphysiological system that merges organoid and organ-on-a-chip technology (achberger2019mergingorganoidand pages 1-3). The platform integrates more than seven hiPSC-derived retinal cell types—including photoreceptors and RPE—in a microfluidic device with vasculature-like perfusion through a hyaluronic acid-based hydrogel (achberger2019mergingorganoidand pages 14-15, achberger2019mergingorganoidand pages 3-5). Critically, this is the first in vitro system to recapitulate the interaction between mature photoreceptor outer segments and RPE, resulting in a three-fold increase in outer segment formation compared to organoids cultured without RPE support (seah2024modelinginheritedretinal pages 14-14). The RoC enables multiple functional readouts amenable to kinetic monitoring: Fura-2-based calcium imaging of photoreceptor responses, quantification of photoreceptor outer segment phagocytosis by RPE (via PNA lectin and rhodopsin co-localization with early endosomes), and time-resolved drug toxicity testing (achberger2019mergingorganoidand pages 11-14, achberger2019mergingorganoidand pages 14-15). The platform successfully reproduced retinopathic side effects of chloroquine and gentamicin, with the RPE layer demonstrating a protective barrier effect evidenced by differential propidium iodide staining (achberger2019mergingorganoidand pages 11-14). Perfusion enables continuous secretome and metabolome sampling, although fabrication complexity limits parallelization relative to plate-based assays (achberger2019mergingorganoidand pages 14-15, achberger2019mergingorganoidand pages 3-5).

### 5. Immunocompetent Choroid-on-Chip (CoC)

Cipriano et al. developed a three-channel microfluidic choroid-on-chip that integrates iPSC-derived RPE, primary microvascular endothelial cells, and melanocytes embedded in a dextran hydrogel, with physiological perfusion of peripheral blood mononuclear cells (PBMCs) to achieve immunocompetence (cipriano2022humanimmunocompetentchoroidonchip pages 2-4, cipriano2022humanimmunocompetentchoroidonchip pages 9-10). The platform recapitulates the outer blood-retinal barrier architecture with tissue dimensions matching in vivo measurements (300 µm vs. 266.8 ± 78.0 µm), and demonstrates controlled immune cell recruitment with in vivo-like cytokine release profiles (cipriano2022humanimmunocompetentchoroidonchip pages 2-4, cipriano2022humanimmunocompetentchoroidonchip pages 4-6). For therapeutic monitoring, the CoC enables continuous effluent cytokine profiling, immune cell trafficking quantification, and pharmacological response ranking—as demonstrated by the immunosuppressive effects of cyclosporine A and immune activation by CD3 bispecific antibodies (cipriano2022humanimmunocompetentchoroidonchip pages 4-6, cipriano2022humanimmunocompetentchoroidonchip pages 8-9). This model is particularly suited for evaluating inflammatory and immune-mediated aspects of AMD and ocular drug safety, though it is less optimized for direct mitochondrial aging endpoints.

### 6. Outer Blood-Retinal Barrier (oBRB)-on-Chip with Integrated TEER Sensing

Several groups have developed microfluidic oBRB models incorporating RPE and endothelial cells separated by porous membranes, with integrated transepithelial electrical resistance (TEER) electrodes for continuous, non-invasive barrier integrity monitoring (caragnano2025biologicalbarriermodelsonchips pages 8-10). The electrode placement on glass substrates beneath PDMS layers provides flexible, non-interfering continuous measurements, making this approach particularly amenable to kinetic monitoring of barrier disruption under hypoxic or metabolic stress conditions (caragnano2025biologicalbarriermodelsonchips pages 8-10). Complementary readouts include immunofluorescence for tight junction proteins (ZO-1, occludin, claudins) and permeability assays (barar2026bridgingpreclinicaland pages 24-25, wu2022tissueengineeringin pages 7-9). Wet-AMD-on-a-chip variants additionally incorporate perfusable three-dimensional vascular networks and have been validated with dose-dependent bevacizumab responses (barar2026bridgingpreclinicaland pages 24-25). These platforms are well suited for screening barrier-protective and anti-angiogenic therapeutics, though many currently employ the ARPE-19 cell line rather than patient-specific hiPSC-RPE (caragnano2025biologicalbarriermodelsonchips pages 8-10, caragnano2025biologicalbarriermodelsonchips pages 6-8).

### 7. Cigarette Smoke Extract (CSE)-Treated hiPSC Retinal Organoids for Modeling Dry AMD Photoreceptor Degeneration

Vergara et al. developed a hiPSC-derived retinal organoid model in which mature organoids are treated with cigarette smoke extract (CSE, 500–750 µg/ml) to recapitulate key aspects of dry AMD-associated photoreceptor degeneration (vergara2026humanretinalorganoid pages 1-2). CSE exposure induces oxidative stress with reactive oxygen species accumulation primarily in the outer nuclear layer, mitochondrial membrane depolarization (detected by JC-1 staining), and dual activation of ferroptosis (characterized by intracellular iron accumulation via FerroOrange, lipid peroxidation via BODIPY 581/591-C11, and glutathione dysregulation) and intrinsic apoptosis (cleaved caspase-3 and -9 activation) (vergara2026humanretinalorganoid pages 6-9, vergara2026humanretinalorganoid pages 4-6, vergara2026humanretinalorganoid pages 4-4). Proteomic analysis identified 159 differentially expressed proteins enriched in ferroptosis pathways (greater than 20-fold enrichment), redox regulation, and neurodegeneration (vergara2026humanretinalorganoid pages 4-6). Critically, the study validated multiple live fluorescence-based assays—ethidium homodimer, PSVue-794, dihydroethidium, JC-1, and BODIPY—for quantifying photoreceptor death and stress in live organoids, providing a toolbox for kinetic drug screening (vergara2026humanretinalorganoid pages 2-4, vergara2026humanretinalorganoid pages 10-11). However, the model lacks RPE juxtaposition, retinal vasculature, and microglia (vergara2026humanretinalorganoid pages 9-9).

### 8. Microfluidic Puck for Time-Resolved Polarized RPE Secretion

The microfluidic "Puck" developed by Spivey et al. is designed specifically for time-resolved interrogation of polarized RPE cell responses (spivey2023amicrofluidicplatform pages 1-3). By mounting pre-polarized Transwell RPE monolayers within a 7.6-µL microfluidic chamber, the device achieves a 200–350-fold volume reduction compared to standard Transwells, dramatically increasing the concentration of low-abundance secreted factors and improving spatiotemporal detection sensitivity (spivey2023amicrofluidicplatform pages 3-4). Under hypoxic conditions (7% O₂), the system detected significant increases in basal VEGF secretion (from 1.40 to 1.68 ng/mL) within two hours, while apical VEGF remained constant, demonstrating true kinetic monitoring of polarized secretion dynamics (spivey2023amicrofluidicplatform pages 4-6, spivey2023amicrofluidicplatform pages 1-3). TEER measurements (≥500 Ω·cm²) confirm monolayer integrity during perfusion, and the AlphaLISA immunoassay enables near-real-time nanomolar detection (spivey2023amicrofluidicplatform pages 3-4). The platform can support tens of parallel experiments and is envisioned as a quality-control tool for iPSC-RPE prior to transplantation (spivey2023amicrofluidicplatform pages 6-7, spivey2023amicrofluidicplatform pages 7-9).

### 9. Patient-Derived iPSC-RPE Oxidative Stress Drug Screening

Early work by Chang et al. demonstrated that RPE cells derived from dry AMD patient iPSCs exhibit inherently reduced antioxidative defense and elevated endogenous ROS compared with control RPE, making them more susceptible to hydrogen peroxide-induced injury (chang2014thegenerationof pages 5-7). This patient-specific oxidative stress model served as an expandable drug screening platform, identifying curcumin as the most effective protective agent among several antioxidant candidates (chang2014thegenerationof pages 5-7). While simpler than organ-on-a-chip approaches, this paradigm established the feasibility of personalized AMD drug testing using patient iPSC-derived RPE.

In summary, no single model currently captures all aspects of aging macula pathology while simultaneously providing high-throughput kinetic monitoring. For direct assessment of mitochondrial dysfunction and metabolic stress, automated hiPSC-RPE monolayers with Seahorse bioenergetic profiling offer the highest throughput and quantitative rigor (truong2021automatinghumaninduced pages 6-8, truong2021automatinghumaninduced pages 14-20), while nutrient microenvironment optimization further refines the disease relevance of metabolic phenotypes (lim2026nutrientmicroenvironmentsreprogram pages 1-5, lim2026nutrientmicroenvironmentsreprogram pages 21-24). For multicellular complexity, the retina-on-a-chip platform of Achberger et al. uniquely recapitulates the photoreceptor-RPE interaction with calcium imaging and phagocytosis readouts (achberger2019mergingorganoidand pages 11-14, achberger2019mergingorganoidand pages 1-3), and the immunocompetent choroid-on-chip adds the inflammatory/immune dimension (cipriano2022humanimmunocompetentchoroidonchip pages 2-4, cipriano2022humanimmunocompetentchoroidonchip pages 1-2). For long-term aging phenotypes including drusen formation, aged RPE organoids provide unmatched longitudinal fidelity over months to a year (mesch2021agedhumanipscrpe pages 1-4, mesch2021agedhumanipscrpe pages 7-9). For oxidative stress-induced photoreceptor degeneration specifically, CSE-treated retinal organoids with validated live fluorescent assays provide the closest approximation to dry AMD photoreceptor death mechanisms (vergara2026humanretinalorganoid pages 6-9, vergara2026humanretinalorganoid pages 2-4). Finally, for time-resolved secretion dynamics and barrier function, the microfluidic Puck (spivey2023amicrofluidicplatform pages 4-6, spivey2023amicrofluidicplatform pages 1-3) and TEER-integrated oBRB chips (caragnano2025biologicalbarriermodelsonchips pages 8-10) offer the strongest kinetic monitoring capabilities. An ideal strategy for comprehensive therapeutic efficacy assessment would combine complementary platforms—for example, Seahorse-based mitochondrial profiling on automated RPE monolayers for primary screening, followed by validation in retina-on-a-chip or CSE-treated organoid systems with live fluorescence readouts for secondary mechanistic confirmation.
